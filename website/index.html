Key Findings

What we discovered

01
Drug class is the dominant zero-shot signal
A zero-parameter heuristic achieves MRR 0.280 (65× random) purely from drug class membership. Genes that resist known beta-lactams resist novel beta-lactams. This is biology, not learning — and it sets the bar every model must beat.
02
R-GCN works — and we know exactly why
Per-relation weight matrices give R-GCN a dedicated learned function for each of the 9 edge types. The antibiotic→class pathway propagates drug-class identity to unseen antibiotics cleanly. Every other GNN collapses all edge types into one aggregation, diluting this signal.
03
AUC is a misleading metric for this task
HGT: 84.8% AUC — 0% Hits@10. GraphSAGE: 87.9% AUC — 1.3% Hits@10. High AUC reflects node-type discrimination, not antibiotic-level ranking. Filtered MRR over all 231 antibiotics simultaneously is the correct metric.
04
KGE models fail zero-shot by construction — regardless of architecture
ComplEx (BRIDGE's best model, 97% transductive accuracy) scores Hits@10 = 0% in our setting. Proved with a controlled experiment: the same DistMult model achieves 68.2% AUC on seen antibiotics and 49.5% (random) on unseen. The failure is structural — not a hyperparameter problem.
