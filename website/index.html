Key Findings

What the data tells us

01
Drug class is the dominant zero-shot signal
A zero-parameter heuristic achieves MRR 0.280 (65× random) purely from drug class membership. Genes that resist known beta-lactams will resist novel beta-lactams. This is biology — not learning. It sets the bar every trained model must clear.
02
R-GCN works — and the reason is architectural
Per-relation weight matrices give R-GCN a dedicated learned function for each of the 9 edge types. The antibiotic→class pathway propagates drug-class identity to unseen antibiotics cleanly. Every homogeneous GNN collapses all 9 edge types into one aggregation, diluting the drug-class signal beyond recovery.
03
AUC is a misleading metric for this task
HGT achieves 84.8% AUC — with 0% Hits@10. GraphSAGE achieves 87.9% AUC — with 1.3% Hits@10. High AUC reflects node-type discrimination, not actual antibiotic ranking ability. Filtered MRR over all 231 antibiotics simultaneously is the only honest metric for this task.
04
KGE models fail zero-shot by construction — architecture is irrelevant
ComplEx is BRIDGE's best model at 97% transductive accuracy. In our setting: Hits@10 = 0% across all 5 seeds. Controlled proof: same DistMult model scores 68.2% AUC on seen antibiotics, collapses to 49.5% (random) on unseen. The failure is structural — lookup-table embeddings are never updated for entities with no training edges.
